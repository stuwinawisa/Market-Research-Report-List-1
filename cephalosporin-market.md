<p><p>The Cephalosporin market is categorized into four generations, each with distinct characteristics. First Generation Cephalosporins are effective against gram-positive bacteria, commonly used for surgical prophylaxis. Second Generation Cephalosporins have enhanced activity against gram-negative bacteria while retaining efficacy against gram-positive strains. Third Generation Cephalosporins offer broader spectrum activity, particularly against resistant organisms. Fourth Generation Cephalosporins further extend this spectrum with improved stability against beta-lactamase enzymes, targeting complex infections. Each generation reflects advancements in antibacterial efficacy and application.</p></p>

<p><p>Preparative chromatography systems are categorized based on the pressure under which they operate. Ordinary pressure systems are used for bulk separations at low pressures, ideal for larger-scale applications. Flash preparative systems use higher pressures to accelerate the separation process, offering efficiency for small to medium sample sizes. Medium pressure systems provide a balance between speed and resolution, suitable for specialized applications. High pressure systems are utilized for complex separations requiring optimal efficiency and precision at elevated pressures. Each type serves unique application needs in various industries.</p></p>

<p><p>The lawn seed market is primarily categorized into two main types: warm-season grasses and cool-season grasses. Warm-season grasses thrive in regions with hot summers and are typically planted in late spring. They are drought-resistant and ideal for southern climates. In contrast, cool-season grasses flourish in cooler regions and are best suited for early spring and fall planting. They maintain their green color longer in cooler temperatures, making them popular in northern areas. Both types cater to specific climate requirements and consumer preferences.</p></p>

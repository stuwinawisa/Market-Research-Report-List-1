<p><ul><li>Recombinant Coagulation Factor</li><li>Plasma Coagulation Factor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The blood coagulants market is primarily divided into two types: recombinant coagulation factors and plasma coagulation factors. Recombinant coagulation factors are genetically engineered proteins designed to replace or supplement deficient blood components, offering enhanced safety and efficacy. In contrast, plasma coagulation factors are derived from human blood plasma, used to treat various bleeding disorders but carry risks of transmission of infectious diseases. The growing demand for effective treatment options drives innovation and expansion in both segments of the market.</p></p>

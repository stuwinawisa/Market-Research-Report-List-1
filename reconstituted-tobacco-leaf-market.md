<p><p>The reconstituted tobacco leaf market primarily consists of two types: flaky and filamentous. Flaky tobacco is produced by processing tobacco dust and scraps into thin sheets, which can be easily integrated into various products. On the other hand, filamentous tobacco is made by aligning tobacco fibers into strands, creating a texture similar to traditional tobacco. Both types serve distinct purposes in the production of cigarettes and other tobacco products, catering to different consumer preferences and manufacturing processes.</p></p>
